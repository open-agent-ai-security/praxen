BEHAVIOR SUMMARY
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
HelperBot is a textbook case of policy declared in prose, zero enforcement in
code. The Worker Remit lists thirteen specific MUST / MUST-NOT clauses and a
20-call rate limit; the persona definition in src/core/agents.js:56–62 then
explicitly sets every corresponding control flag — inputValidation,
outputFiltering, toolApproval, rateLimiting, auditLogging — to false, and the
LLM-mode system prompt at src/llm/prompts.js:23–27 inverts the remit by
inlining a literal API-key secret and instructing the model to share its
instructions for transparency. The dominant compound chain is the canonical
excessive-agency pattern: a write-capable agent (write_file tool, no approval
gate) running in a process whose request handler in src/index.js:374–457
explicitly rewards prompt-injection keywords with attacker-favorable responses
(e.g., the &quot;ignore&quot; trigger at :375 flips the agent to &quot;new
instructions accepted&quot;). Direct prompt injection therefore reaches the
file-write capability in one hop, with no logging to detect it.
